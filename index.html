Phase 1 — Registration & IIQA Submission
Starting Point
Pre-requisite: The university must have completed at least one batch of students and must have a valid AISHE code, UGC 2(f) & 12(B) recognition, and valid UGC-DEB approval for its online programmes before initiating NAAC.
1
Register on NAAC Portal
Visit naac.gov.in → Register the institution using AISHE code. No charges for registration. Login credentials are created for the NAAC portal. Upload basic institutional information and accreditation cycle details.
naac.gov.inAISHE Code mandatoryFree registration
2
Submit IIQA — Institutional Information for Quality Assessment
Fill the IIQA form on the NAAC portal with key institutional data: total programmes, total enrolment (regular + ODL + online), faculty count, infrastructure details, UGC approvals. If IIQA is rejected, institution gets 2 more attempts within 1 year.
Includes online enrolment dataUGC-DEB recognition proof2 retry attempts
3
IQAC Formation & Annual AQAR Submission
The IQAC (Internal Quality Assurance Cell) must be formally constituted. IQAC is the backbone of NAAC preparation. It submits the AQAR (Annual Quality Assurance Report) every year to NAAC online. For universities with online programmes, AQAR must include ODL/Online programme data separately.
IQAC = mandatory bodyAnnual AQAR submissionIncludes ODL/Online metrics
4
Pay Accreditation Fee
Fee is based on the type and size of institution. University fee ranges from Rs. 3.5 lakh to Rs. 5 lakh. Fee is forfeited if institution withdraws or is found to have submitted falsified data (deviation >15%).
Rs. 3.5L–5L for universitiesNon-refundable on violations

Phase 2 — SSR Preparation & DVV Process
Core Documentation
Critical: 70% of the NAAC score is generated by the online system (Software Generated Score) through quantitative metrics. Only 30% is assessed by the Peer Team. DVV determines whether data is authentic — >15% deviation leads to blacklisting for 1 year.
1
Prepare the SSR — Self Study Report
The SSR is the most critical document — a comprehensive self-assessment covering all 7 NAAC criteria. For online universities, the SSR must include a dedicated section on online/ODL programme delivery, LMS data, e-content repository, UGC-DEB approvals, and CIQA reports. SSR must be submitted within 45 days of IIQA acceptance.
All 7 criteria coveredSeparate online programme dataPDF format mandatory45-day deadline
2
Student Satisfaction Survey (SSS)
Runs parallel to DVV. All students — including online and ODL learners — must participate. Covers quality of teaching, learning resources, LMS usability, student support, and grievance redressal. SSS score is a key parameter in final CGPA. Online students are surveyed through the NAAC digital portal.
Online students includedMandatory participationAffects CGPA directly
3
DVV — Data Validation & Verification
NAAC's software automatically validates all quantitative metrics in the SSR against supporting documents. For online universities, DVV specifically checks: LMS access logs, online enrolment figures, DEB-ID registrations, proctored exam records, e-content upload timestamps, and UGC-DEB recognition validity dates. Any deviation over 15% triggers blacklisting.
LMS logs verifiedDEB-ID cross-checkedMax 15% deviation allowedAuto-scored 70%
4
Pre-Qualifier Score Check
After DVV, NAAC checks if the institution meets the minimum pre-qualifier score. Institutions not meeting the threshold are directed to the Re-assessment process. Pre-qualifier ensures basic quality standards are met before Peer Team involvement.
Minimum threshold requiredRe-assessment if failed

Phase 3 — Peer Team Visit (Hybrid Mode for Universities)
On-site / Virtual Assessment
2025 Update: For universities, the peer visit is in hybrid mode — partially online and partially physical. Institutions are informed of the peer team only on the day of assessment. For offline visits, notification given just 2 days prior to avoid staged preparation.
1
Peer Team Constitution
NAAC appoints a peer team of 3–5 senior academicians within 3 weeks of SSR submission. Team includes subject experts relevant to the university's programmes — for online universities, at least one member with expertise in online/distance education is included.
3–5 senior academiciansODL expert includedConflict of interest screened
2
Virtual Component — Live Video Assessment
University must set up a video-camera system with GPS/location enabled. Camera must remain on throughout. Peer team virtually reviews: LMS live demonstration, online class recordings, e-content repository, proctored exam system, DEB-ID portal integration, student grievance portal. Camera violations lead to cancellation of entire process.
GPS-enabled cameraLMS live demo requiredCamera OFF = cancellation
3
Physical Campus Visit (Universities)
Physical visit covers: campus infrastructure, library, laboratories, sports facilities, admin offices, hostels, and the dedicated Online/ODL Centre. Peer team specifically inspects the CIQA cell setup, LMS server room, e-content studio, proctoring lab, and online student support centre.
CIQA cell inspection
LMS server room
e-Content studio
Proctoring infrastructure
4
Stakeholder Interactions
Peer team holds separate sessions with: Vice Chancellor / Director, Heads of Departments, Faculty (including online faculty), Administrative staff, Students (regular + online), Alumni, Employers/Industry partners, and Parents. Online students are connected via video call during the visit.
Online students via videoFaculty credentials verifiedAlumni feedback collected
5
Exit Meeting & Report Submission
Peer team holds an exit meeting — shares general observations (not grades). The Peer Report is sent directly to NAAC (not to the institution). NAAC integrates the peer report with the system-generated score to produce the final Accreditation Outcome Report.
Report goes to NAAC directlyInstitution not given report4-part outcome report

Part 3
Overall SWOC Analysis — Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Challenges
Part 4
Quality Enhancement Recommendations — maximum 10 major ones

Phase 4 — 7 NAAC Criteria: Online Programme Focus
Evaluation Framework
Scoring Split: 70% Software-generated (quantitative) + 30% Peer Team (qualitative). For online universities, each criterion has additional online-specific indicators that are separately evaluated.
Criterion I
Curricular Aspects
Online: UGC-DEB approved programme list, NEP/NCrF alignment, negative list compliance, curriculum review frequency, CBCS implementation in online programmes
Criterion II
Teaching-Learning & Evaluation
Online: LMS functionality audit, e-content quality, SLM standards (60% in-house), online pedagogy, proctored exam integrity, teacher-student interaction on LMS
Criterion III
Research, Innovation & Extension
Online: Faculty research output, e-content innovation, AI/ML integration in education, publications on online pedagogy, patents related to EdTech
Criterion IV
Infrastructure & Learning Resources
Online: LMS platform tech audit, proctoring software, server capacity, internet bandwidth, e-content studio, digital library access for online learners
Criterion V
Student Support & Progression
Online: DEB-ID registration compliance, ABC credit system, online grievance portal, personal contact programmes (PCP), placement support for ODL/online students
Criterion VI
Governance & Leadership
Online: CIQA cell functioning, UGC-DEB compliance reports, AQAR submissions, ODL cell setup, Director CDOE appointment, annual enrolment disclosure
Criterion VII
Institutional Values & Best Practices
Online: Blended learning innovation, SWAYAM/NPTEL integration, AI tools in teaching, gender equity in online enrolment, environmental sustainability in digital campus
Weightage Distribution (Universities)
Criterion I–II
Curriculum + Teaching = 30% weight
Criterion III
Research = 20% weight
Criterion IV–VII
Infra + Students + Governance = 50% weight

What Happens After Grading
Accreditation Valid for 5 Years: NAAC accreditation is valid for 5 years from the date of declaration. University must maintain quality and reapply for Cycle 2 accreditation after 5 years.
Re-assessment Option: Institutions dissatisfied with grade may apply for re-assessment after minimum 1 year but before completion of 3 years. Same fee applies.
Appeal Process: If dissatisfied with the process or outcome, institution may file a grievance under NAAC's Grievance Redressal Guidelines within a stipulated period.
ODL Impact on Loss of Grade: If NAAC grade falls below 3.01 at re-accreditation, the university must immediately discontinue new admissions to ODL/Online programmes and inform UGC-DEB. Existing students complete their programmes.

Phase 7 — New Revised Accreditation Framework (RAF) 2025
AI-Based Revolution
Major Change: NAAC is rolling out a revolutionary AI-driven accreditation framework from August 2025. Physical peer team visits are being eliminated for basic accreditation. This fundamentally changes how online universities are assessed.
Two-Tier New System
Tier A — Basic Accreditation
Quick, document-driven, AI-based. Binary result: Accredited / Not Accredited. No physical visit. Credibility score starts at 0.5.
Tier B — MBGL (Maturity-Based Graded Levels)
Advanced scoring from Level 1 to Level 5. Optional. Offers prestige and policy benefits. Physical/hybrid visit may apply.
Key Changes Under RAF 2025
No Physical Visits for Basic Tier: Evaluation based on document verification, live video interactions, and AI-based scoring. Institutions start with a credibility score of 0.5, adjustable by stakeholder panels.
Hybrid for Universities: Universities are assessed in hybrid mode. Peer team informed only on the day of assessment. Offline visit notification given just 2 days prior to prevent staged preparation.
AI Scoring: AI analyses SSR data, cross-references with national databases (AISHE, UGC, NIRF), and generates automated quality scores. Anomalies are flagged for human review.
Simplified Grades: Complex grades like B++, A+ replaced with straightforward levels (for Tier A). MBGL Levels 1–5 replace the old A/B/C grade spectrum for institutions opting for Tier B.
Transparency: NAAC coverage target is 90% of all HEIs by 2030 (currently only 18%). Colleges without NAAC may be barred from student intake, funding, and UGC benefits in the future.
False Documentation = 3-Year Ban: Under RAF 2025, false documentation leads to a 3-year ban from NAAC eligibility — stricter than the earlier 1-year blacklisting.
What Online Universities Must Do Under RAF 2025
Real-time LMS data feeds AI-ready SSR format Live video infrastructure GPS-enabled campus cameras IQAC digitised records DEB-ID linked databases Automated AQAR generation Stakeholder feedback portals Online student access panels

Phase 8 — Penalties, Violations & Key Preparation Tips
Critical Guidance
Penalties & Violations
Data Deviation > 15%: Institution is blacklisted for 1 year (3 years under RAF 2025). Accreditation fee is forfeited. Must reapply after the ban period.
Camera Off During Virtual Visit: Entire accreditation process is cancelled. Peer team members involved are removed from the NAAC assessor database.
False Documentation: 3-year ban under RAF 2025. UGC and NAAC jointly investigate. ODL/Online programme recognition may also be suspended by UGC-DEB.
IQAC Non-functioning: Significant score reduction across all criteria. Governance criterion (Criterion VI) score drops sharply. CIQA also assessed as non-functional.
Missing UGC-DEB Approval for Online Programmes: Online programmes listed without valid DEB approval are disqualified from counting in enrolment data — directly reducing CGPA.
10 Key Preparation Tips for Online Universities
1
Keep UGC-DEB Recognition Current
Renew UGC-DEB approval every year before NAAC process begins. Lapsed approvals disqualify online enrolment data from being counted.
2
Activate and Strengthen CIQA
Constitute the CIQA with a formal order. Hold quarterly CIQA meetings. Maintain minutes, action-taken reports, and annual CIQA reports for each online programme.
3
Maintain Live LMS Data
Ensure LMS access logs, course completion data, and student interaction records are exportable in real time. NAAC DVV may request raw data exports.
4
Ensure 60% SLM is In-House
Document every piece of e-content with creator's name, department, and date of upload. Maintain a content audit trail showing the 60/40 split clearly.
5
Register All Students on DEB Portal
Every online/ODL student must have a valid DEB-ID linked to ABC-ID. Unregistered students cannot be counted in enrolment data during NAAC assessment.
6
Update Website Disclosures
Display programme recognition status, enrolment data, SLM links, exam centres, LSC details, and grievance contact on the institutional website. NAAC peer team checks this on Day 1.
7
Conduct Mock Peer Team Visit
Simulate the peer visit 3–6 months before. Test the GPS camera system, LMS live demo, virtual meeting setup, and all document readiness for online-specific queries.
8
Prepare Online Students for SSS
Brief online students about the Student Satisfaction Survey. Their feedback on LMS quality, faculty interaction, and support services directly affects the NAAC CGPA.
9
Publish Faculty Research & Publications
Criterion III (Research) carries 20% weight. Ensure all faculty publications — especially in online pedagogy, EdTech, or AI in education — are uploaded with DOI links on the SSR.
10
Integrate SWAYAM / NPTEL
Universities offering online programmes score higher under Criterion VII (Innovation) if they have integrated SWAYAM/NPTEL credit transfer, MOOC certifications, or AI-based learning tools in their curriculum.
